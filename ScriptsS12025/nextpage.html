In chess, the King moves one square in any direction, the Rook moves horizontally or vertically any number of unobstructed squares, and the Bishop moves diagonally any number of unobstructed squares. The Queen combines the Rook and Bishop's movement, while the Knight jumps in an 'L' shape, and the Pawn moves one square forward (or two on its first move) and captures diagonally.
